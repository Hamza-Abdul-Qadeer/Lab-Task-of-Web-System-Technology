History & Development of PU

The University of the Punjab is one of the oldest and largest universities in Pakistan. It was established in 1882 in Lahore and has played a major role in the development of higher education in the country. The university offers a wide range of academic programs in sciences, arts, humanities, business, and technology.Punjab University has many departments and affiliated colleges across Punjab. It is known for its strong academic traditions and has produced many famous scholars, researchers, and leaders.Established during the British colonial period, the University of the Punjab was the first university in a Muslim-majority area of South Asia. It began with a focus on liberal arts and sciences and grew into a comprehensive institution encompassing over 125 departments and institutes. Its history is closely tied to the educational evolution of the subcontinent and Pakistan’s nation-building after 1947.

  • Founded: 1882, Lahore, British India
  • Type: Public research university
  • Campuses: 10 (including Quaid-e-Azam, Allama Iqbal, Gujranwala, Jhelum)
  • Students: Over 54,000 on-campus
  • Affiliated Colleges: More than 500

Academic structure and research

PU offers more than 400 degree programs through faculties such as Arts and Humanities, Science, Engineering, Social Sciences, Business, and Law. It runs notable research centers including the Centre of Excellence in Molecular Biology (CEMB) and the Centre for South Asian Studies. The university supports innovative research in biotechnology, environmental science, and heritage studies, contributing significantly to Pakistan’s academic output.
