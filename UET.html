Historic Profile

The University of Engineering and Technology (main) located in the northern part of Lahore on the historical "Grand Trunk Road (G.T. Road)", near the magnificent Shalimar Gardens built during the great Mughal Empire.The institution started its career in 1921 as the ‘ Mughalpura Technical College ’. Later it became the ‘Maclagan Engineering College’, a name given to it in 1923 when Sir Edwards Maclagan, the then Governor of the Punjab who laid the foundation stone of the main building, now called the Main Block, which still retains its majesty despite the wear and tear of over eight decades. At that stage the institution offered courses of study in only two disciplines; Electrical Engineering and Mechanical Engineering.
  • Founded: 1921 (as Mughalpura Technical College)
  • Charted: 1961 by the Government of Punjab
  • Type: Public research university
  • Main Campus: Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan

Academics and Research

The university houses seven faculties and more than 35 departments offering undergraduate, master’s, and doctoral programs across engineering, architecture, computer science, and management. It maintains 19 specialized research centers, including those focused on renewable energy, artificial intelligence, and nanotechnology. UET is also home to ESUPAK, a consultancy and engineering services unit supporting public and private sectors.

Ranking & Recognition

UET Lahore consistently appears among South Asia’s top engineering institutions in QS and regional rankings, recognized for its century-long contribution to engineering education and national development.

Impact & Alumni

With more than 74,000 alumni worldwide, UET Lahore has contributed significantly to Pakistan’s industrial and technological growth. Its graduates hold leadership positions in academia, government, and global technology firms. The UETians Lahore Endowment Foundation (ULEF USA) continues to strengthen this legacy by funding scholarships, innovation labs, and AI training initiatives.
